Digital Evidence Portal

Small Claims

 

What is the Digital Evidence Portal?

  1. The Digital Evidence Portal is a software application that allows Small Claims litigants to upload, share, view, and present evidence for a specific hearing.
  2. Evidence uploaded to the Digital Evidence Portal is securely stored and will be available on your hearing date to present; eliminating the need to bring copies of your evidence to the Court.

Upload Your Evidence

  1. You may upload your evidence if you are a party in a Small Claims matter and will present evidence at the hearing. The use of the Digital Evidence Portal is not mandatory, however, you are strongly encouraged to upload your evidence to the portal.

Getting Started — How to Use the Digital Evidence Portal

How do I create a digital evidence account?

  1. First, be sure to have the following information available:
    • Case number
    • Name of party to the case
    • Hearing date (this must be a future or current date)
    • Your email address or cell phone number
  2. Next, on the portal's landing page, you will first click on Sign-Up. Then, you will need to identify your case by the case number and hearing date.
  3. Once your case has been found, follow the prompts to finalize your account creation.
  4. Learn how to create an account, as well as how to upload and share evidence by watching the following tutorial: Watch Video Tutorial

Am I required to share evidence?

  1. Yes, if you are a party to the case that wishes to submit evidence in support of your respective position, you are required to provide copies of the evidence to the opposing side at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date.
  2. If you upload your evidence to the digital portal, you may share the evidence electronically without having to mail a physical copy to the Court and the opposing side.

What are the benefits of using the digital evidence portal?

  1. The digital evidence portal allows the litigant to:
    • Find their case(s) and upload images and documents electronically for the case(s).
    • Time stamp evidence uploaded to the system.
    • Share the evidence with the opposing party.
    • Download the evidence.

I will be presenting evidence at the hearing. By when do I have to upload the evidence?

  1. Any evidence you plan on offering to support your position must be shared with the opposing party and the Court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing.
  2. In lieu of mailing to the Court and to the opposing party, evidence may be uploaded via the digital evidence portal for cases with a future hearing date.
  3. All evidence must be uploaded and shared through the day of the hearing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to upload all of my evidence at one time?

No, evidence may be uploaded in multiple sessions. For the Court to consider the evidence, all items must be uploaded at least ten (10) days prior to the scheduled hearing date.

What if I do not upload and share my evidence at least 10 days prior to the hearing?

Pursuant to the operative Standing Order re: Evidence Exchange, a party's failure to share evidence at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.

What if I don't have sufficient contact information to share the evidence with the opposing side?

Parties can:

  • Upload evidence to the portal for the benefit of themselves and the Court.
  • Mail evidence to the opposing party according to the Exchange and Submission of Evidence (Small Claims) LASC local form LACIV278.
Can I still submit evidence in person?

A party's failure to submit evidence to the Court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.

I am a business and file multiple cases. Do I need to create a separate account for each case?

No, a separate Digital Evidence account is not required. You may upload to different cases as long as you are able to provide the case type, case number and future hearing date.

I have created a Court ID to register for Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) and LACourtConnect (LACC). Can the same account be used to upload my digital evidence?

No, a unique account will be created for the Digital Evidence Portal.

If there are multiple plaintiffs and/or multiple defendants, does each party need to upload evidence?

No, parties may upload evidence individually or upload evidence jointly.

24. How long will the Court store my digital evidence?

The Court will not have access to the evidence after the ruling. However, evidence will not be purged from the system until 60 days from the notification date.

25. Will I be notified if the Court intends to purge my digital evidence?

The litigant that uploaded the evidence will receive a notification providing at least 60 days prior to Court's intent to purge digital evidence from the portal. During the 60-day period, you may access and download any item desired.

26. Can I receive my evidence after the hearing has concluded?

Yes. The Digital Evidence Portal allows you to download your evidence up to 60 days after the case has been completed. After 60 days, the evidence will be purged from the portal. If the evidence is not downloaded during this time frame, it will be lost.
