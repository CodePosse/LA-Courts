What is the Digital Evidence Portal?

The Digital Evidence Portal is a cloud-based software application that allows Small Claims litigants to upload, view, share, and present evidence for a specific hearing. Evidence uploaded to the Digital Evidence Portal is securely stored in a cloud portal and will be available on your hearing date to present; eliminating the need to bring evidence to the Court.

Getting Started — Using the Digital Evidence Portal

What is the Digital Evidence Portal?

The Digital Evidence Portal is an online system that allows litigants in Small Claims to submit their evidence online. The portal allows the litigant to do the following:

  1. Find their case(s) and upload evidence in the format of images and documents for the case(s).
  2. Time stamp evidence uploaded to the system.
  3. Share the evidence with the other party.
  4. Download the evidence.

About the Digital Evidence Portal

What information is required to create a Digital Evidence account? - in PPT
a. Before uploading evidence to the portal, the party must create an account, using the case type, case number and future hearing date. The Digital Evidence Portal will validate the information. If found to be accurate, it will permit you to create a user account and profile consisting of your name, email address or cell phone number.
Am I required to share my evidence?- in PPT
a. Yes, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 116.520, parties that wish to submit evidence to support their respective position must provide a copy to the opposing side.
b. If you upload your evidence to the digital portal, you may share without the need to mail a copy to the court and the opposing side.
How do I upload my evidence to the portal? - in PPT
a. The following guide provides steps to upload and share evidence using the portal
b. Add Digital Evidence Portal button.
I will be presenting evidence at the hearing, by when do I have to upload the evidence? - in PPT
a. Standing Order 2020-SJ-015-01 requires that a copy of any evidence the party wishes the court to consider should be submitted to the court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date.
b. In lieu of mailing, evidence may be uploaded via the Digital Evidence Portal for cases with a future hearing date.
c. All evidence must be uploaded and shared through the day of the hearing.
Do I need to upload all of my evidence at one time? - in PPT
a. No, evidence may be uploaded at any time prior to the hearing date.
What if I do not upload and share my evidence at least 10 days prior to the hearing? [policy] - in PPT
b. Pursuant to Standing Order XXXX, a party's failure to share evidence at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.
PENDING - What if I don't have sufficient contact information to share the evidence with the opposing side?
Policy decision on Court's receipt of evidence.
a. Upload evidence to the portal
b. Mail evidence to the opposing party.
c. The claim form includes mailing address Form 278 (outlines protocol). Mailing address is requirement.
Can I still submit evidence in person? - in PPT
a. A party's failure to submit evidence to the Court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.
I am a business and file multiple cases. Do I need to create a separate account for each case? - in PPT
a. No, a separate Digital Evidence account is not required. You may upload to different cases as long as you are able to provide the case type, case number and future hearing date.
I have created a Court Id to register for Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) and LACourtConnect (LACC). Can the same account be used to upload my digital evidence?
a. No, a unique account will be created for the Digital Evidence Portal.
If there are multiple plaintiffs and/or multiple defendants, does each party need to upload evidence?
a. No, parties may upload evidence individually or upload evidence jointly.
