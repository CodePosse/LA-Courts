Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the Digital Evidence Portal?

  1. The Digital Evidence Portal is a cloud-based software application that allows Small Claims litigants to upload, share, view, and present evidence for a specific hearing.

2. What are the benefits of uploading my evidence?

  1. Evidence uploaded to the Digital Evidence Portal is securely stored in a cloud portal and will be available on your hearing date to present; eliminating the need to bring copies of your evidence to the Court.

3. What information is required to create a Digital Evidence account?

  1. Before uploading evidence to the portal, the party must create an account, using the case type, case number and future hearing date. The Digital Evidence Portal will validate the information. If found to be accurate, it will permit you to create a user account and profile consisting of your name, email address or cell phone number.

4. Am I required to share my evidence? -

  1. Yes, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 116.520, parties that wish to submit evidence to support their respective position must provide a copy to the opposing side.
  2. If you upload your evidence to the digital portal, you may share without the need to mail a copy to the court and the opposing side.

6. I will be presenting evidence at the hearing, by when do I have to upload the evidence?

  1. Standing Order 2020-SJ-015-01 requires that a copy of any evidence the party wishes the court to consider should be submitted to the court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date.
  2. In lieu of mailing, evidence may be uploaded via the Digital Evidence Portal for cases with a future hearing date.
  3. All evidence must be uploaded and shared through the day of the hearing.

7. Do I need to upload all of my evidence at one time?

  1. No, evidence may be uploaded in multiple sessions. In order for the Court to consider the evidence, all exhibits must be uploaded at least ten (10) days prior to the scheduled hearing date.

8. What if I do not upload and share my evidence at least 10 days prior to the hearing? [policy]

  1. Pursuant to Standing Order XXXX, a partys failure to share evidence at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.

10. Can I still submit evidence in person?

  1. A partys failure to submit evidence to the Court at least ten (10) days prior to the hearing date may result in the evidence not being considered by the Court.

11. I am a business and file multiple cases. Do I need to create a separate account for each case?

  1. No, a separate Digital Evidence account is not required. You may upload to different cases as long as you are able to provide the case type, case number and future hearing date.

12. I have created a Court Id to register for Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) and LACourtConnect (LACC). Can the same account be used to upload my digital evidence?

  1. No, a unique account will be created for the Digital Evidence Portal.

13. If there are multiple plaintiffs and/or multiple defendants, does each party need to upload evidence?

  1. No, parties may upload evidence individually or upload evidence jointly.

14. What does it mean to upload evidence jointly?

  1. When there are multiple defendants, one party can upload evidence on behalf of more than one party. (24.2 joint account enhancement).

After the Hearing

24. How long will the Court store my digital evidence?

  1. The Court will not retain the evidence after the ruling on the case.

25. Will I be notified if the Court intends to purge my digital evidence?

  1. Prior to purging digital evidence from the portal, the litigant that uploaded the evidence will receive an email (?cell phone) notice providing at least 60 days advanced notification.

26. Can I receive my evidence after the hearing has concluded?

  1. Yes. The Digital Evidence Portal allows you to download your evidence up to 60 days after the case has been completed. After 60 days, the evidence will be purged from the portal. If the evidence is not downloaded during this time frame, it will be lost.
