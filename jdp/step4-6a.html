Parking

YOU MUST BRING YOUR JURY SUMMONS OR JUROR BADGE TO ACCESS FREE PARKING.

Free juror parking is provided in the parking structure located near the front entrance of the building. Entrance to the parking structure is on Pacific Concourse. Show your jury summons or badge to the attendant for entry.

Jurors must bring in their summons or badge to enter and exit the parking structure. Jurors must scan their summons/badge bar code at the bar code scanner located at the parking entrance or exit. Jurors will only be allowed to enter and park for free if they have a summons or badge, and their group number has been called in to report for service.

Note: Maximum overhead clearance for the facility is 8'2". Any vehicle exceeding the maximum overhead clearance will be directed by an attendant to a suitable parking area.

Other Information

Jurors may proceed to the front of the weapon screening line with a jury summons or badge. A cafeteria is located on the first floor of the building and vending machines are located in the jury assembly room.

Orientation Video

The law requires that you participate in the orientation process each time you are summoned for jury service to ensure you understand your role and responsibilities as a trial juror. You must complete the orientation prior to reporting for jury service.

Completing the orientation process online requires that you view the orientation video in its entirety. The orientation process takes approximately 9 minutes to complete.

Please note that the orientation video is hosted on YouTube. If you experience difficulties playing the video, it may be because:

  • the YouTube site is down,
  • your browser does not support YouTube, or
  • the YouTube site is blocked or restricted on your device.

If you are not able to complete the online orientation for any reason, you may do so by:

  • Calling the Court's Serve Jury telephone system at (213) 972-0970. After entering your JID and PIN numbers, select the orientation option. The recording is approximately 5 minutes in length and you must listen to the entire recording to receive credit.
  • Reading the orientation script included in the mailing with your summons prior to reporting for service.
